Immersions and Embeddings

In short, immersion + homeomorphism = embedding. When the inclusion map is an embedding, there exists submanifolds.

Definition [Immersion,Embedding] Let MmM^mMm and NnN^nNn be differentiable manifolds. A differentiable mapping φ:M→N\varphi: M\rightarrow Nφ:M→N is said to be an immersion if dφp:TmM→Tφ(p)Nd\varphi_p:T_mM\rightarrow T_{\varphi(p)}Ndφp​:Tm​M→Tφ(p)​N is injective for all p∈Mp\in Mp∈M. If M⊂NM\subset NM⊂N and the inclusion i:M⊂Ni:M \subset Ni:M⊂Nis an embedding, we say that MMM is a submanifold of NNN.

Proposition [Relation between immersion and embedding] Let φ:M1n→M2m\varphi:M_1^n\rightarrow M_2^mφ:M1n​→M2m​, n⩽mn\leqslant mn⩽m, be an immersion of the differentiable manifold M1M_1M1​ into the differentiable manifold M2M_2M2​. Comments: This shows that every immersion is locally (in a certain sense) an embedding.

Topology of Manifolds

Definition [Locally Finite] Let MMM be a differtiable manifold. A family of open sets {Uα}\{U_\alpha\}{Uα​} is said to be locally finite if for every p∈Mp\in Mp∈M there exists a neighborhood VVV of ppp such that VVV intersects only finitely many sets UαU_\alphaUα​.

  3. ∑αfα(p)=1\sum_\alpha f_\alpha(p) = 1∑α​fα​(p)=1 for all p∈Mp\in Mp∈M. (This makes sense since for eahc ppp, only finitely many fαf_\alphafα​ are non-zero.)
